ether/propylene carbonate electrolyte

Keywords:
Lithium metal battery
Lithium dendrites
SEI ﬁlm
Electrodeposition
Coulombic efﬁciency

It is so far an efﬁcient way for depressing dendrite growth to adjust the electrolyte composition. Therefore, we
study the electrochemical behavior of lithium metal electrodeposited in a LiPF6-propylene carbonate (PC) electrolyte by adding two kinds of crown ether. The effect of crown ether concentration on the Coulombic efﬁciency of lithium electrodes has be evaluated, and the electrodeposition behavior of lithium metal on
negative electrode has be analyzed by cyclic voltammetric curve, electrodeposition curve and lithium metal
deposition morphology. The results show that a certain amount of crown ether (15-crown-5 and 12-crown4) can preferentially coordinate with lithium ion in the electrolyte and form complex compounds, which
reduce the lithium ions concentration in the “hot spot” and level the lithium electrode greatly by inhibiting
the preferential growth of lithium dendrite. A uniform and dense nano-columnar lithium layer could be
obtained at the current density of 2.0 mA cm−2 when the deposition amount is 1.0 mAh cm−2, and the concentration of 15-crown-5 and 12-crown-4 ether are 0.3 M and 0.2 M, respectively. Gaussian calculation results
show that the lithium-crown complex has a relatively lower lowest unoccupied molecular orbit (LUMO), where
electrons could be injected during the charging process, leading to decreased Coulumbic efﬁciency.

2. Experiment
2.1. Chemical reagents and raw materials
Most of the chemical reagents including 15-crown-5, 12-crown-4
ether and 1 M LiPF6-PC are purchased from Aladdin Biochemical Technology Co., LTD. The polyethylene (PE) separator is bought from
Sinoma Science & Technology Co. Ltd., China. In the glove box (H2O < 0.1 ppm, O2 < 0.1 ppm, Mikrouna Co., Shanghai, China),
0.4 ml, 0.8 ml, 1.2 ml, 2.0 ml 15-crown-5 (or 12-crown-4 ether) were
added into 20 ml 1 M LiPF6-PC commercial secondary electrolyte in an
high-purity argon atmosphere, respectively. In the aluminum reagent
bottle, the solution was fully mixed for 24 h stirred by a magnetic agitator. Then, and the 15-crown-5 (or 12-crown-4 ethers) electrolyte
with concentrations of 0.1 M, 0.2 M, 0.3 M and 0.5 M were obtained,
respectively. The Uv–vis absorption spectrum of the electrolyte was
measured by Shimadzu 3600 Uv–vis spectrophotometer.
2.2. Electrodeposited technology
Copper foil as the cathode (Φ12 mm, working electrode), lithium
metal as the anode (Φ 16 mm, control electrode), and Ag/Ag+ as reference electrode (internal liquid: acetonitrile solution with 0.01 M
AgNO3), the three-electrode system is constructed in a sealed electrolyte pool. The constant current electrodeposition is carried out by
CHI 660C (Chenhua Co., Shanghai, China) in chronopotential method,
with ﬁxed capacity of 1.0 mAh cm−2 in current density of
2.0 mA cm−2. The Cyclic Linear Sweep Voltammetry is also tested
by CHI 660C, with a potential range of 0–3.3 V (vs Li/Li+) and a scanning rate of 5 mV s−1.
2.3. Battery assembly
The standard 2032 type Li/Cu half-cell is assembled in the glove
box full of Ar. From the positive shells to negative shells are Cu foil,
electrolyte 35 μl, PE separator, electrolyte 35 μl, lithium foil, gasket
and the shrapnel. LAND multi-channel battery testing system (Land
Co., Wuhan, China) was used for battery testing, with copper foil as
work electrode for lithium metal deposition/stripping (corresponding
to a battery charge/discharge process). The current density is
0.5 mA cm−2 and 1.0 mA cm−2 in 0.5 mAh cm−2 and 1.0 mAh
cm−2 respectively with the voltage range of 0.5–1.0 V.

3.4. Electrochemical behavior of lithium metal in crown ethers electrolyte
solution
Fig. 9a and 9b are the voltage curves of the lithium deposition process in the electrolyte without crown ether and 0.3 M 15-crown-5
ether, respectively. In the control group, lithium ions tend to exist in
the free form in the electrolyte. Due to the microscopic defects on
the electrode surface and the presence of tiny synapses, more negative
charges tend to accumulate in the hot spot. The hot spot area has
greater electric ﬁeld strength and can attract more lithium ions in
the electrolyte. Therefore, the lithium metal is deposited faster here,
forming synapses. And the positive feedback effect intensiﬁes the
growth of lithium synapses, thereby the growth of lithium dendrites.
After adding a certain amount of crown ether to the electrolyte, due
to the strong coordination effect of crown ether, lithium ions tend to
be existed in the form of crown ether-Li+ complex. On the basis of
the original three steps including mass transfer, electrochemical reaction and electrocrystallization, the complex dissociation is also added
to the cathodic process, which further elevates the charging voltage
[29,30]. On the other hand, this process will also slow down the
growth rate of lithium at the tip of the synapse, thereby inhibit the
growth of lithium dendrites and level the lithium metal anode [31].
Fig. 10 is the molecular orbital energy levels of 15-crown-5 ether
and lithium-ion complexes with 15-crown-5 ether calculated by the
Gaussian software package (12-crown-4 ether results are similar).
The inset pictures present the electron cloud for the HOMO and LUMO
levels of the compounds. 15-crown-5 ether had a higher HOMO (v.s S.
H.E) energy and a lower LUMO (v.s S.H.E) energy before the complex
was formed, indicating that the compound was easy to be oxidized but
difﬁcult to be reduced. When the complex was formed, its LUMO
decreased signiﬁcantly, indicating that the compound could easily
obtain electrons to form electronically neutral complex molecular radicals and even negatively charged complex complex ions. When the
negative electrode is charged in the electrolyte containing the complex
positive ion, these reduced species are no longer attracted by the acceleration of the electric ﬁeld force and thus unable to be discharged near
the negative electrode. Instead, the electrically neutral free radicals
will diffuse freely into the solution. The negatively charged negative
ions accelerate away from the surface of the negative electrode, diffusing to the counter electrode and discharging at the positive electrode,
which results in a decrease in CE. This diffusion is not only related to
the concentration of crown ether complex ions, that is, the larger the
concentration is, the faster the decline will be, but also related to

4. Conclusion
In this paper, the inﬂuence of two crown ether including 15-crown5 and 12-crown-4 ether as complexing agents on the morphology of
lithium metal electrodeposition is investigated, and the electrodeposition behavior of Li metal after the addition of crown ethers are studied.
A certain amount of 15-crown-5 or 12-crown-4 ether added to 1 M
LiPF6-PC electrolyte can effectively level the lithium layer deposited
and inhibit the growth of lithium dendrite, thus obtaining a uniform
and dense nanometer lithium deposit layer at 2.0 mA cm−2. The optimal concentration of 15-crown-5 complexing agent is 0.3 M, and the
optimal concentration of 12-crown-4 complexing agent is about
0.2 M. However, the deposition potential of lithium metal is increased
when crown ether was added, and the dissolution/ deposition CE of
Li/Cu half battery lithium is also decreased. We consider that crown
ether seem to inhibit the formation of passivation layer on the surface
of lithium metal, resulting the lower the CE. On the basis of the
